March 20th 2026

Nowrouz Pirouz

The spring equinox arrives, marking another cycle of rebirth. Nowrouz—the \"New Day\"—has been celebrated for over 3,000 years, a testament to humanity's eternal hope for renewal.

In the darkness of winter, we plant seeds of intention. As the light returns, so does our capacity for transformation. The Haft-sin table becomes a meditation on life itself: sprouts for rebirth, apples for health, garlic for medicine, vinegar for age and patience.

The Liminal Space

This year, I find myself contemplating the space between endings and beginnings. The moment before the vernal equinox, when the old year hasn't quite released its grip, and the new hasn't fully arrived. That liminal space where anything is possible.

Nowrouz Pirouz—may the new day be victorious.
March 24th 2026

Dark Music Inspirations

Music has always been my gateway to the unconscious. The darker, more atmospheric compositions speak to something primal—a frequency that bypasses rational thought and communicates directly with the soul.

Lately, I've been immersed in the works of artists who understand that silence is as important as sound. The spaces between notes, the decay of reverb into void, the tension of an unresolved chord.

The Elegance of Darkness

These sonic landscapes inform my visual work, my writing, my understanding of beauty. There's an elegance in darkness that the light can never capture—not because darkness is superior, but because it reveals what brightness obscures.

The best art exists in the liminal spaces: between light and dark, between sound and silence, between meaning and mystery.
